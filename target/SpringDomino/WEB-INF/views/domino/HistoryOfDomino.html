The history of the domino game

Domino is a board game in which a chain of knuckles ("bones", "stones") touching halves with the same number of dots denoting the number of points is built.

The roots of the game of dominoes go back to China, it was there that bones appeared in the form of plates with red and white dots. The local, original version of domino is still widely popular there.

In the eighteenth century, the game was brought to Italy and modified. The word domino, which the game was called in Italy, is the same root as the Italian (Ital. domino) and the Latin (Latin dominus) word meaning Lord. There are several versions of why it happened. Fasmer believed that this is due to the fact that the one who first lays out all the dice becomes domino, i.e. "master". According to another version, the game of dominoes, unlike other gambling games, was allowed in Catholic monasteries, and there, as a rule, any business began with "praising the Lord."
